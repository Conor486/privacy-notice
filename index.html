6. ADDITIONAL TERMS FOR JOB SEEKERS

AI-Powered Matching Engine and Transparency

By providing a Resume and Profile information, you understand and agree that the Platform will use artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms to analyze your information and help identify Employers and Listings that may be a good fit for your skills, location, and background.

AI System Information

Our matching and ranking systems employ the following AI technologies:

  • Résumé Parser (BERT-NER): Extracts structured data from CVs and resumes
  • Matching Engine (Siamese BERT): Matches candidates to roles using semantic analysis
  • Candidate Ranking (XGBoost): Scores and ranks candidates based on multiple factors

Fairness and Bias Mitigation

These AI systems undergo:

  • Quarterly fairness testing using demographic parity, equal opportunity, and disparate impact ratio metrics

  • Human oversight and validation as described in our AIIA framework

Transparency Commitments

You have the right to:

  • Understand how AI decisions affect your matching and ranking
  • Request information about the logic and factors influencing your matches
  • Contact us at support@scalis.ai or ai-governance@scalis.ai with questions about AI-driven decisions

EMPLOYER DATA PROCESSING ADDENDUM

Version 1.2

This Employer Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between Scalis LLC ("Scalis") and you (as an "Employer") that incorporates this DPA by reference (the "Agreement"). This DPA governs the processing of Employer Personal Data by Scalis in providing its job-matching platform (the "Platform") pursuant to the Agreement.

Scope: This DPA solely applies to Employer Personal Data, meaning Personal Data an Employer provides through the Platform. In those limited situations, Scalis acts as a processor of Employer Personal Data on your behalf (as controller).

1. DEFINITIONS

  • "Applicable Data Protection Law" means applicable law governing the processing of Personal Data, including U.S. Data Protection Laws and European Data Protection Laws.
  • "Automated Decision-Making" means processing of Personal Data for evaluating personal aspects of a natural person, which produces legal effects concerning them or similarly significantly affects them.
  • "Candidate Data" means Personal Data relating to an Employer's job seekers, candidates, or applicants, which is a subset of Employer Personal Data.
  • "Security Incident" means a confirmed breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.

2. PROCESSING OF EMPLOYER PERSONAL DATA

2.1. Roles of the Parties

Employer is the controller and Scalis is the processor of Employer Personal Data.

2.2. Scalis's Processing

Scalis will process Employer Personal Data only to provide the Platform in accordance with the Agreement and this DPA. The details of processing are in Appendix A.

2.3. Prohibited Use

Important: Scalis shall not retain, use, or disclose Employer Personal Data, including Candidate Data, for analytics, profiling, AI model training, or any other secondary purpose beyond providing the Platform as instructed by the Employer, unless expressly authorized in writing by the Employer.

3. SECURITY AND CONFIDENTIALITY

3.1. Technical and Organizational Measures

Scalis shall implement and maintain the technical and organizational measures described in Appendix B.

3.3. Security Incident Response

Upon becoming aware of a Security Incident, Scalis will notify you without undue delay, and in any event not later than 72 hours, and will take reasonable steps to mitigate the effects of the Security Incident.

4. ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE AND AUTOMATED DECISION-MAKING

4.1. Transparency and Enablement

Where the Platform uses Automated Decision-Making to process Candidate Data, Scalis shall provide the Employer with sufficient information about the logic involved and the capabilities of the Platform to enable the Employer to meet its transparency and fairness obligations under Applicable Data Protection Law and EEOC guidance.

4.2. Human Oversight

Scalis shall ensure the Platform includes features that enable the Employer to conduct a human review of decisions made through Automated Decision-Making.

4.3. Opt-Out Rights

The Platform shall include functionality to enable the Employer to honour any data subject's request to opt out of Automated Decision-Making where required by law.

4.4. Bias Mitigation

Scalis commits to regularly monitoring its AI models for unlawful bias and will take reasonable steps to mitigate any such bias detected.

4.5. Candidate Transparency Support

Scalis shall provide reasonable assistance to the Employer to meet transparency obligations towards candidates, including providing standardized explanations of the logic, significance, and intended consequences of its Automated Decision-Making processes.
