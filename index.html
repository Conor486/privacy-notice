Summary of Terms and Principles

Scalis observes applicable privacy and data protection law in the markets we serve, when processing personal data. This includes European Union's and United Kingdom's General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR"), GDPR variations in the European Economic Area, US States' privacy law and other data protection law as required.

Generally, personal data are data that can be directly or indirectly traced back to, and identify, a natural person, referred to as a "data subject."

Personal data processing can refer to anything we might do with personal data, such as collecting, recording, organizing, structuring, storing, updating, or modifying, retrieving, consulting, using, providing by transmission, distributing, making available, aligning, combining, shielding, deleting, or destroying.

Scalis observes core principles when processing personal data. The data:

  • are processed lawfully, properly, and transparently;
  • are processed only for the purpose for which they were collected (purpose limitation);
  • are processed as little as possible (data minimization);
  • are processed correctly (accuracy of data);
  • will not be kept longer than necessary (storage and retention limitation);
  • are transferred internationally under applicable international regulations;
  • are processed with safeguards in technical and organizational controls to address security, integrity, and confidentiality; and
  • are processed in systems, programs and services with privacy and security by design.

How Scalis Uses Artificial Intelligence

Our AI assists recruiters and candidates by analyzing text and structured data. AI supports—but does not replace—human decision-making. We do not use facial recognition, emotion detection, or biometric categorization.

AI Processing Activities

The table below describes how we use AI in our Platform:

Module Purpose Technology Human Oversight
Résumé analysis & matching Identify skills/experience that match job requirements Natural Language Processing (NLP), semantic vector embeddings Recruiters review shortlists before outreach
Candidate ranking / scoring Suggest a numerical "fit score" (0–100) Machine-learning classifiers (e.g., decision trees / gradient boosting) Human review and override required
Interview summarization Generate text summaries of interviews Transformer-based language models Human validation before record is stored

Meaningful Information About the Logic Involved

Our models evaluate patterns aligned to the role (skills, tenure, recency, similarity to requirements) to produce a recommendation for recruiter review. Final hiring decisions are made by humans.
